

   Which Cusped Census Manifolds Fiber?
	by Nathan Dunfield <nathan@dunfield.info>
	Jan 16, 2015.  

For each of the 4815 manifolds cusped hyperbolic 3-manifolds in the
Callahan-Hildebrand-Weeks census which comes with SnapPea, I tried to
determine whether or not they fibered over the circle.  I found:

 4198 manifolds which fiber (87.19%).
  452 manifolds which do not fiber (9.39%).
  165 manifolds where I could not determine whether they fibered (3.43%).

Two techniques were used:

  First, most of these manifold have fundamental groups which
  2-generator, 1-relator presentations.  For such groups, Kenneth S. Brown 
  gave a very nice algorithm to compute the Bieri-Neumann-Strebel
  invariant.  This BNS invariant completely determines whether the
  manifold fibers or not. 

  Second, Marc Lackenby's notion of a "taut ideal triangulation" gives
  a way of certifying that a cusped 3-manifold fibers over the circle, by
  finding an orientation of the 2-skeleton of an ideal triangulation
  which satisfies certain conditions.  Note that this method only
  shows that a manifold fibers and not vice versa.

Most of the fibered manifolds are certified to fiber by both of these
two tests. As a sanity check that I had correctly implemented both
algorithms, I ran them independently on the census manifolds and then
compared results.  As for the remaining manifolds where I did not
determine whether they fibered, my presumption is that they do not.
Probably in all but perhaps a handful of cases, this could be easily
checked by computing the Alexander Polynomial, but I have not bothered
to do so.  The total running time for the BNS algorithm is a minute or
so, and for the taut ideal triangulation stuff about 8 hours.

NOTE: Prior to 2003/8/10 the table data was incomplete.  In
particular, some manifolds were not listed at all.  This was because
the script that produced the table failed to include manifolds which
where known to fiber only via the method of taut triangulations.  The
underlying data has not changed, and there were no errors in earlier
versions of this document except those of omission.
